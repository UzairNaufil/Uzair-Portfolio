Who I am

An architect of AI-directed systems.

I'm a student at Aptech Karachi, enrolled in the 3-year ACCP AI program. But my real education happens outside the classroom — building, shipping, and breaking things with AI.

My approach is AI-directed development: I define the problem, architect the solution, and direct AI to generate the code. Then I run a multi-AI review pipeline — having separate models audit each other's output for security vulnerabilities and logical gaps before anything ships. I don't write code manually. I build systems.

I've shipped two real-world applications using Claude, Groq API, Ollama, Supabase, and WhatsApp Cloud API. Both solve genuine problems for real users — not academic exercises.

My vision

Karachi deserves to be on the AI map.

0
Official Claude community ambassadors in Pakistan right now

Karachi has no Claude community. That is the opportunity — and the reason I'm building one.

I'm surrounded by passionate, ambitious builders at Aptech who are hungry to work with AI but have no structured space to come together, share knowledge, or collaborate. I want to create that space — somewhere builders in Karachi can gather for free, grow together, and actually ship things.

Pakistan risks being left behind in the global AI revolution — not because of a lack of talent, but because of a lack of community infrastructure. I want to fix that in my city.

My first step: a hands-on AI builders session at Aptech. No slides, no theory. Attendees bring an idea, we build it with Claude in a single session. From there — monthly meetups, and eventually a Karachi AI hackathon.
